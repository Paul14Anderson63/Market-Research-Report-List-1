<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Wear Resistant Steel is classified into three types based on its hardness levels, namely Under HBW 400, HBW 400-500, and Above HBW 500. Under HBW 400 refers to wear-resistant steel with a hardness level of less than 400 on the Brinell Hardness scale. HBW 400-500 indicates steel with a hardness range between 400 and 500. Above HBW 500 signifies wear-resistant steel with a hardness level exceeding 500. These classifications help identify the specific properties and applications of the steel, allowing manufacturers to choose the appropriate type for their intended use.</p></p>
