<p><p>Breast lesion localization devices are used to accurately locate abnormal areas of the breast for surgical procedures. There are four main types of localization devices: </p><p>1. Wire Localization: A thin wire is inserted into the breast to mark the exact location of the lesion before surgery.</p><p>2. Radioisotope Localization: A radioactive material is injected into the breast, which accumulates in the abnormal area and is detected by a gamma probe during surgery.</p><p>3. Magnetic Tracer: Small magnetic markers are placed into the breast, and a handheld magnetic probe is used to locate and guide the surgeon to the targeted lesion.</p><p>4. Others: This category includes emerging technologies or alternative methods for lesion localization, such as fluorescent dyes or ultrasound-guided techniques.</p></p>
