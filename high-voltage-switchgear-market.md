<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The high-voltage switchgear market is divided into two types: Gas Insulated Switchgear (GIS) and Air Insulated Switchgear (AIS). GIS uses a gas such as sulfur hexafluoride to insulate electrical components, allowing for compact designs and efficient operation. It is commonly used in urban areas where land is limited. On the other hand, AIS utilizes air as its insulation medium, making it suitable for outdoor installations. It is commonly used in remote or rural areas where space is not a constraint. Both types play a crucial role in the distribution of electricity.</p></p>
