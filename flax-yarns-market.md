<p><p>Flax yarns are produced through two market types: wet spinning and dry spinning. Wet spinning involves immersing the flax fibers in a chemical solution to make them more flexible before spinning. This method produces strong and fine yarns suitable for high-quality fabrics. On the other hand, dry spinning doesn't involve any chemical treatment and directly spins the flax fibers. It results in coarser and less flexible yarns, commonly used for industrial applications such as twine and cordage. Both wet spinning and dry spinning offer different characteristics and cater to varying market demands.</p></p>
