<p><p>Real-time PCR (qPCR) and Digital PCR (dPCR) are molecular biology techniques used to amplify and quantify specific segments of DNA in a sample. They both utilize PCR (polymerase chain reaction) to amplify DNA, but differ in the way the amplified DNA is detected and quantified.</p><p>Real-time PCR (qPCR) involves the detection of amplified DNA in real-time as the reaction progresses. It uses fluorescent dyes or labeled probes to measure the accumulation of the amplified DNA during each cycle of PCR. This method provides quantitative data on the initial amount of DNA present in the sample.</p><p>Digital PCR (dPCR), on the other hand, partitions the sample into thousands or millions of individual reactions. PCR amplification occurs in each partition independently, and the presence or absence of amplification is detected after the reaction is complete. This method provides absolute quantification of the target DNA in a sample.</p><p>The Real-time PCR (qPCR) and Digital PCR (dPCR) Market is expected to experience substantial growth in the coming years. Factors such as the increasing prevalence of infectious diseases, advancements in molecular biology techniques, and the growing demand for personalized medicine are driving the market growth.
