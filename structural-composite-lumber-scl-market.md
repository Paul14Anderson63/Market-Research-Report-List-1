<p><p>Structural Composite Lumber (SCL) refers to a category of engineered wood products used in construction and furniture making. It encompasses four main market types: Laminated Veneer Lumber (LVL), Laminated Strand Lumber (LSL), Parallel Strand Lumber (PSL), and Oriented Strand Lumber (OSL). LVL is made by bonding veneer sheets, providing strength and versatility. LSL is formed by adhering long wood strands together, ensuring increased strength and stability. PSL consists of long and parallel veneer strands for maximum structural performance. OSL utilizes randomly oriented strands to offer strength and stiffness throughout the product.</p></p>
