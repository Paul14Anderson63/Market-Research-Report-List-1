<p><p>Activated carbon for mercury control is a key technology used to reduce harmful emissions of mercury from industrial processes, primarily in coal-fired power plants. Activated carbon has the ability to adsorb and remove mercury from flue gas streams, making it an integral component of air pollution control systems.</p><p>The global market for activated carbon for mercury control is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. The demand is driven by stringent environmental regulations worldwide, which mandate the reduction of mercury emissions. The United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) has set standards for mercury emissions from power plants, leading to the adoption of advanced air pollution control technologies such as activated carbon systems.</p><p>The market analysis indicates that the growth of the activated carbon for mercury control market is also driven by the increasing installation of coal-fired power plants in emerging economies, where coal continues to be a dominant source of energy. PAC offers several advantages, including higher adsorption capacity, higher surface area, and easier handling and dosing. This trend is expected to drive the demand for PAC in the market.</p><p>Overall, the activated carbon for mercury control market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.8% during the forecast period.
