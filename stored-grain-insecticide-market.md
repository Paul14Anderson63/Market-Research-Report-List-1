<p><p>The stored grain insecticide market encompasses various types of insecticides used to protect grains from insect infestation during storage. Organophosphates are chemicals that target the nervous systems of insects, while pyrethroids affect their nerve cell function. Methyl carbamates work by inhibiting the enzymes necessary for insects' survival. Neonicotinoids are systemic insecticides that affect the central nervous system. Bio-insecticides are derived from natural substances and provide environmentally friendly pest control. The "Others" category includes alternative insecticides with different mechanisms of action, such as insect growth regulators.</p></p>
