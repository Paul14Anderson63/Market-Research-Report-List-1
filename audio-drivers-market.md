<p><p>The audio drivers market comprises various types of speakers that produce different ranges of frequencies. Tweeters reproduce high-frequency sounds, such as cymbals or vocals. Midrange drivers handle frequencies between tweeters and woofers, producing instruments' sounds. Woofers and subwoofers handle lower frequencies, providing bass and deep tones. Super tweeters go beyond tweeters, extending high-frequency range. Crossovers are devices that divide audio signals between different drivers to enhance sound quality. These different types of audio drivers cater to various frequency ranges, offering a complete and immersive audio experience.</p></p>
