<p><p>The bio-based polyurethane market is categorized into three types based on their flexibility: flexible, semi-rigid, and rigid. </p><p>Flexible bio-based polyurethanes are highly elastic and adaptable, making them suitable for applications that require flexibility, such as textiles, coatings, adhesives, and foams. </p><p>Semi-rigid bio-based polyurethanes have moderate flexibility and stiffness, making them ideal for applications that require a balance between flexibility and rigidity, such as shoe soles, automotive parts, and packaging.</p><p>Rigid bio-based polyurethanes are stiff and inflexible, making them appropriate for applications that require high strength and durability, such as construction materials, insulation panels, and electronic components.</p></p>
