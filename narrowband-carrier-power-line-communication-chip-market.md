<p><p>Narrowband Carrier Power Line Communication (PLC) chips can be categorized into two market types: Narrowband Low Speed and Narrowband High Speed. Narrowband Low Speed PLC chips are designed for applications that require lower data transmission rates, such as home automation systems or smart meters. They operate at lower frequencies, typically ranging from a few kilohertz to several hundred kilohertz. On the other hand, Narrowband High Speed PLC chips are designed for applications that require higher data transmission rates, such as high-definition video streaming or advanced industrial automation. They operate at higher frequencies, typically ranging from several hundred kilohertz to several megahertz.</p></p>
