<p><p>Scintillators are materials that emit light when exposed to radiation and are used in various industries for radiation detection applications. The market for scintillators can be categorized into three types: solid scintillator, liquid scintillator, and gaseous scintillator. Solid scintillators are rigid materials that offer high stability and efficiency. Liquid scintillators consist of a liquid solvent that provides better radiation detection capabilities. Gaseous scintillators, on the other hand, use gases as the scintillating medium and are often preferred for large-scale, high-energy physics experiments due to their fast response time.</p></p>
