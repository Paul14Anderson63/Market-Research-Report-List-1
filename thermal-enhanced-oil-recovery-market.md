<p><p>Thermal Enhanced Oil Recovery (TEOR) is a method employed in the oil industry to extract crude oil from reservoirs with low permeability. The most commonly used techniques for TEOR include steam flooding, cyclic steam stimulation, and in-situ combustion.</p><p>The Thermal Enhanced Oil Recovery Market is witnessing significant growth and is expected to continue expanding at a CAGR of 8% during the forecast period. One of the key drivers behind this growth is the increasing global energy demand, coupled with the depletion of easily accessible conventional oil reserves. As conventional oil extraction methods become less effective, there is a growing need for enhanced oil recovery techniques like TEOR.</p><p>Moreover, the rising crude oil prices have made unconventional and heavy oil resources economically viable, further fueling the demand for TEOR solutions. Many oil companies are investing in TEOR projects to recover oil from unconventional reserves, such as oil sands and shale oil.</p><p>In terms of geographical analysis, North America is expected to dominate the thermal enhanced oil recovery market due to the presence of vast reserves of heavy oil and oil sands in countries like Canada and the United States. <p><p>Thermal Enhanced Oil Recovery (TEOR) refers to various techniques used to extract oil from reservoirs by increasing its temperature. The TEOR market is divided into different types of chemicals used in this process. Polymers refer to substances that help in trapping and carrying oil to the production well. Surfactants reduce interfacial tension between oil and water, facilitating oil flow. Alkaline chemicals neutralize acids present in the reservoir, enhancing oil recovery.
