<p><p>Packaged air conditioners refer to HVAC systems that are pre-packaged and contain all the necessary components for cooling a specific area. There are two types of packaged air conditioners based on the condenser used: water-cooled condenser and air-cooled condenser. </p><p>Packaged AC with water-cooled condenser utilizes water as a cooling medium to remove heat from the refrigerant. The condenser transfers heat from the refrigerant to the water, which is then circulated and cooled in a separate cooling tower or a body of water.</p><p>On the other hand, packaged AC with air-cooled condenser uses ambient air to cool down the refrigerant. The condenser transfers heat from the refrigerant to the surrounding air. This type of system does not require additional water sources or cooling towers.</p><p>Both types of packaged air conditioners offer efficient cooling solutions for different environments, with water-cooled condenser systems being more suitable in areas with ample water resources and air-cooled condenser systems being more versatile and easier to install.</p></p>
