<p><p>The 2D semiconductor materials market comprises three main types: graphene, hexagonal boron nitride (hBN), and transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDs). Graphene is a single layer of carbon atoms arranged in a hexagonal lattice, offering excellent electrical conductivity. hBN is also a hexagonal crystal structure but composed of boron and nitrogen atoms, providing electrical insulation and serving as a protective layer for devices. TMDs are compounds made up of transition metal atoms and chalcogen atoms, displaying semiconducting properties suitable for various electronic applications.</p></p>
