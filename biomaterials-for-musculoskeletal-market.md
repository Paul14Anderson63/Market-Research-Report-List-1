<p><ul><li>Metallic Biomaterials</li><li>Polymeric Biomaterials</li><li>Inorganic Non-Metallic Biomaterials</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The biomaterials for musculoskeletal market comprises metallic biomaterials, polymeric biomaterials, and inorganic non-metallic biomaterials. Metallic biomaterials are made of metals like stainless steel and titanium. They offer high strength and durability but may cause allergic reactions. Polymeric biomaterials are made of synthetic materials like polyethylene and silicone. They are flexible, lightweight, and corrosion-resistant. Inorganic non-metallic biomaterials consist of bioceramics and composites. They possess good biocompatibility but may lack the mechanical strength of metallic biomaterials. These biomaterials play a crucial role in developing implants and medical devices for musculoskeletal disorders and injuries.</p></p>
