<p><p>The non-woven fabric market encompasses various types such as meltblown, spunbonded, spunlace, needle punch, and others. Meltblown fabric is made by melting synthetic fibers and forming a web-like structure, offering high filtration efficiency. Spunbonded fabric involves extruding molten polymer onto a belt and forming continuous filaments, leading to durable and versatile materials. Spunlace fabric is created by entangling fibers through high-pressure water jets, resulting in soft and absorbent fabrics. Needle punch fabric is made by mechanically interlocking fibers using needles, providing excellent strength and dimensional stability. Other types include airlaid, wet-laid, and electrospun fabrics, each with unique characteristics serving various applications.</p></p>
