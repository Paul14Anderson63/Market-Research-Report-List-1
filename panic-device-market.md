<p><p>A panic device, also known as a panic bar or crash bar, is a hardware device installed on exit doors in commercial buildings to allow for quick and easy exit during emergencies. Panic devices are commonly used in places such as schools, offices, hospitals, hotels, and shopping malls.</p><p>The panic device market is projected to experience substantial growth in the coming years. <p><p>The panic device market consists of various types of devices that are used to ensure the safe and efficient evacuation of people during emergencies. Rim exit devices are surface-mounted on doors and are commonly used in commercial buildings. Vertical rod/cable devices have rods or cables that attach to both the top and bottom of the door, providing extra security. Mortise exit devices are installed within the door itself, offering a sleeker appearance. Alarmed exit devices have built-in alarms that sound when the device is activated. There are also other types of panic devices available in the market, each designed to cater to specific needs and requirements.</p></p>
