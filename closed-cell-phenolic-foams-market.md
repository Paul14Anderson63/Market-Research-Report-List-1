<p><p>Closed Cell Phenolic Foams are classified into three types based on their thickness. </p><p>1. Thickness below 40 mm: This type is commonly used in applications where space is limited or where lightweight insulation is required. It is suitable for HVAC systems, pipes, and small-scale thermal insulation projects.</p><p>2. 40 to 80 mm Thickness: This type of phenolic foam provides moderate insulation and is generally utilized in construction applications, such as wall insulation, roof insulation, and floor insulation. It offers excellent fire resistance and thermal conductivity properties.</p><p>3. Thickness above 80 mm: These phenolic foams are typically used for applications requiring high thermal insulation performance. They are commonly employed in industrial settings, refrigeration systems, and cryogenic applications. The foam's thickness enhances its insulation properties, making it efficient in extreme temperature conditions.</p></p>
