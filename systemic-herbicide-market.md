<p><p>A systemic herbicide is a type of herbicide that is absorbed into the plant and transported throughout its tissues, including the roots, stems, and leaves. Unlike contact herbicides that only affect the parts of the plant they come into direct contact with, systemic herbicides are designed to control weeds by killing them from the inside. These herbicides are widely used in agriculture, forestry, and landscaping, as they provide long-lasting and effective control of a wide range of weeds.</p><p>The systemic herbicide market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for high crop yield and the need for efficient weed control methods are some of the factors driving the market growth. <p><p>The systemic herbicide market consists of two main types: selective herbicides and non-selective herbicides. Selective herbicides are designed to target specific types of plants or weeds while leaving desired plants unharmed. They control the growth of unwanted vegetation without causing harm to the desired crop. Non-selective herbicides, on the other hand, are designed to kill or control all types of plants and weeds they come into contact with. They are commonly used in areas where complete elimination of vegetation is required, such as in industrial areas or before planting a new crop.</p></p>
