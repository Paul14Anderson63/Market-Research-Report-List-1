Rolled glass is made by passing the molten glass between rollers, resulting in a textured appearance. Float glass, on the other hand, is made by floating molten glass on a bed of molten tin, creating a smooth and flat surface. Both types of glass are known for their high clarity and low iron content, making them ideal for applications where transparency and color accuracy are important.</p></p>
