<p><p>The Posterior Spinal Fixation Band is an orthopedic device used in spinal surgery to stabilize the spine. It is commonly used in the treatment of spinal deformities such as scoliosis or kyphosis. The fixation band consists of a series of screws and rods that are surgically implanted along the posterior aspect of the spine. These devices provide stability and support to the spine while the patient heals.</p><p>The market for posterior spinal fixation bands has been steadily growing in recent years due to the increasing prevalence of spinal deformities and the advancements in surgical techniques.
