<p><strong>The Aviation Sealant Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Organic</li><li>Inorganic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Aviation sealants can be classified into two main types: organic sealants and inorganic sealants. </p><p>Organic sealants are composed of carbon-based compounds, such as polymers, resins, and elastomers. They offer excellent flexibility, adhesion, and resistance to environmental factors like UV radiation, temperature variations, and chemicals. Organic sealants are widely used in various applications in the aviation industry, such as sealing fuel tanks, windows, and joints.</p><p>Inorganic sealants, on the other hand, are typically made of materials like silicones, polysulfides, and acrylics. They possess high strength, durability, and resistance to extreme temperatures. Inorganic sealants are commonly used in critical areas like engine compartments, exhaust systems, and aerospace structures, where high heat resistance and reliability are crucial.</p><p>These distinct types of aviation sealants cater to different needs and requirements within the aviation industry, ensuring the proper sealing and protection of various aircraft components.</p></p>
