The actuator's primary function is to engage and disengage the brake system by controlling the friction material against the rotating surface of the brake rotor or drum.</p><p>In recent years, the automotive brake actuator market has witnessed significant growth. One key factor driving this growth is the increasing demand for advanced safety features in vehicles. Consumers are increasingly conscious of their safety and are willing to invest in vehicles equipped with advanced braking systems. <p><p>The automotive brake actuator market can be classified into two main types: Alloy and Cast Iron. Alloy brake actuators are made from a combination of two or more metals and offer advantages like lightweight, high strength, and better resistance to corrosion. They are commonly used in modern vehicles to improve overall performance. On the other hand, Cast Iron brake actuators are made from a single material, providing robustness and durability. They are preferred in heavy-duty applications where strength and heat dissipation are crucial factors. Each type caters to different automotive needs and preferences.</p></p>
