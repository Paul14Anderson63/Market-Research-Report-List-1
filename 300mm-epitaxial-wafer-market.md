<p><p>P-type and N-type epitaxial wafers are types of 300mm epitaxial wafers used in the semiconductor industry. P-type wafers are doped with materials that have fewer electrons than the intrinsic silicon, creating a net positive charge. These wafers are used for fabricating electronic devices like transistors. On the other hand, N-type wafers are doped with materials that introduce extra electrons, resulting in a net negative charge. N-type wafers are utilized for manufacturing diodes and other electronic components. The distinction between P-type and N-type wafers lies in the dopant materials used, impacting the wafers' electrical properties and applications.</p></p>
