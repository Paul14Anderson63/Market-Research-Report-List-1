The cooled air is then circulated back to the central unit to be cooled again.</p><p>The central air conditioning market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to various factors. One of the primary drivers is the increasing demand for energy-efficient cooling solutions. <p><strong>The Central Air Conditioning Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Direct Expansion (DX) Central Air Conditioning Systems</li><li>Central Plant/Chilled Water Central Air Conditioning System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Direct Expansion (DX) Central Air Conditioning Systems:</p><p>Direct expansion central air conditioning systems, also known as DX systems, are the most common type of central air conditioning used in residential and small commercial buildings. These systems work by circulating refrigerant directly from the outdoor condensing unit to the indoor cooling coil, which absorbs heat from the air. This cooled air is then distributed through ductwork or individual air handlers to provide cooling throughout the building.</p><p>Central Plant/Chilled Water Central Air Conditioning System:</p><p>Central plant or chilled water central air conditioning systems are typically used in larger commercial buildings or facilities. These systems work by using a central plant to chill water, which is then distributed through piping to various air handling units or fan coils throughout the building. The chilled water absorbs heat from the air and is then circulated back to the central plant to be cooled again. This type of system allows for greater flexibility and zoning capabilities, making it suitable for larger spaces.</p></p>
