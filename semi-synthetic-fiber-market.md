<p><p>The semi-synthetic fiber market includes various types of fibers such as Acetate Fiber, Triacetate Fiber, Vinegar Rayon, and Others. Acetate Fiber is made from cellulose mixed with acetic acid, while Triacetate Fiber is a modified form of acetate fiber with improved properties. Vinegar Rayon is produced by treating cellulose with acetic acid and sulfuric acid. These fibers are commonly used in textile applications due to their durability, comfortable feel, and ability to mimic natural fibers. Other semi-synthetic fibers in the market offer different properties and applications, contributing to the diversity of this industry.</p></p>
