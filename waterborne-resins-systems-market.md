<p><p>Waterborne resin systems refer to a type of coating or adhesive material that is dispersed in water. There are two main types of waterborne resin systems: high molecular weight and low molecular weight. High molecular weight resins have a large number of repeating units and offer enhanced mechanical properties, such as strength and durability. On the other hand, low molecular weight resins have fewer repeating units and provide better flow and adhesion characteristics. These different market types cater to a variety of applications and requirements in industries such as paints, coatings, adhesives, and sealants.</p></p>
