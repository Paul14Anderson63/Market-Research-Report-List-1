<p><p>Concrete fastening systems are used to secure objects to concrete surfaces. Mechanical fastening systems involve the use of bolts, anchors, or screws that are inserted into pre-drilled holes in the concrete, providing a strong and secure connection. On the other hand, adhesive fastening systems rely on adhesives or epoxy resins to bond the object to the concrete surface, providing a reliable and long-lasting attachment. Both types of systems offer different advantages depending on the specific application and load requirements.</p></p>
